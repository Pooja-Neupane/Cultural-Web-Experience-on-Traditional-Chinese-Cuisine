Traditional Chinese Cuisine

A cultural journey through flavors, heritage, and history.

Peking Duck

Beijing (Imperial China)

Crispy, lacquered duck with thin pancakes, scallions, and hoisin sauce — a ceremonial dish of imperial courts.

Discovered: Ming dynasty (14th–17th century)

Mapo Tofu

Sichuan

Silken tofu in a numbing-spicy sauce with Sichuan peppercorns, fermented bean paste, and minced pork.

Discovered: 19th century

Xiao Long Bao

Shanghai

Delicate soup dumplings steamed to perfection with savory broth and seasoned filling.

Discovered: Early 20th century

Kung Pao Chicken

Sichuan

Diced chicken stir-fried with peanuts and chilies — sweet, savory, and spicy with a crunch.

Discovered: Qing dynasty (19th century)
